Example 3 - Arbitrary sub-controllers

This is the Example3Controller.

In the Example3Controller we have a subclass of TemplateController because we don't need it binding anything. What's different with this controller is that it has a subcontroller named 'subexample'.

All controllers (any subclass of Controller) can have any number of (sub)controllers. They are stored in a dictionary an instVar named 'controllers'.

Why this is cool?

Because there is no limit to compose Controllers. You can have controllers with (sub)controllers each with (sub)controllers and compose them again and again. All of them with the right models and using their own templates. You can make them as simple or as complex as you want. A good side-effect of composing controllers is that you keep good Separation of Concerns. In other words... you can scale the complexity of your frontend as much as you want and you'll be just fine.
